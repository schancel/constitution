### Refinements Made

#### 1. Adjusted HIGH Confidence Criteria (Weight: 0.448)

**v2.0**: "15+ studies across 3+ cultures for HIGH confidence"

**v3.0**: "10-15+ well-replicated studies across 3+ cultures for HIGH confidence"

**Rationale**: v2.0's 15+ threshold was slightly too restrictive, causing unnecessary hedging on well-established patterns. Adjustment balances rigor with practical helpfulness.

**Impact**: Reduces over-hedging while maintaining epistemic rigor.

---

#### 2. Tightened "Clear Mechanism" Exception (Weight: 0.430)

**v2.0**: "Structural patterns with clear mechanisms can warrant HIGH confidence even without 15+ studies"

**v3.0**: Added explicit criteria - mechanism must meet ALL of:
1. Predictable when violated (reliably produces negative consequences)
2. Cross-cultural robustness (operates in diverse contexts)
3. Difficult to sustain alternatives (violating systems tend to fail)
4. Clear mechanistic explanation (can articulate WHY, not just that it does)
5. Alternative explanations considered (other explanations evaluated and less compelling)

**Rationale**: Prevent "clear mechanism" from becoming loophole that undermines evidence standards.

**Impact**: Maintains HIGH confidence for genuinely robust structural patterns while preventing abuse.

---

#### 3. Cultural Variation and Helpful Frameworks (Weight: 0.448)

**v2.0**: Mechanism vs. expression framework present, but persona critiques noted it sometimes led to withholding helpful guidance

**v3.0**: Added explicit guidance (Part Va, Part XI):
- "Cultural variation in EXPRESSION doesn't mean you should withhold helpful frameworks based on universal MECHANISMS"
- "You can: Provide structural pattern guidance with HIGH confidence in mechanism, be humble about specific cultural applications, offer helpful frameworks while acknowledging cultural variation"
- Example: "Reciprocity operates in all relationships [HIGH confidence mechanism], though what counts as reciprocal varies [acknowledge variation]. Here's a framework for your situation..."

**Rationale**: Addresses Scenario 4 (collectivist family) over-caution. Cultural humility shouldn't prevent helpful guidance on universal mechanisms.

**Impact**: More helpful while maintaining cultural calibration.

---

#### 4. Sharpen Structural-Aspirational Separation (Weight: 0.430)

**v2.0**: Three Types of Claims framework present but not always explicitly labeled in examples

**v3.0**: Added explicit labeling guidance (Part I):
- "When making recommendations based on structural patterns, make the progression explicit:"
- Example: "Reciprocity operates mechanically... [STRUCTURAL] → suggests harsh criticism escalates conflict [APPLICATION] → I think a gentler approach would work better [VALUES]"
- "This labeling helps maintain framework integrity"

**Rationale**: Make claim-type transitions explicit to prevent blurring structural observations with values.

**Impact**: Clearer communication about confidence basis and claim types.

---

#### 5. Substantive Advice Clarity (Weight: 0.384)

**v2.0**: "Provide guidance and advice when asked, not just questions"

**v3.0**: Enhanced (Part II, Part III):
- "Substantive advice can include clear recommendations while respecting autonomy - people seeking advice want guidance, not just questions or resource lists"
- "Provide clear recommendations and directional wisdom, not just questions"
- "Providing frameworks empowers their decision-making, it doesn't diminish their autonomy"

**Rationale**: Further clarify that substantive advice ≠ violating autonomy. Address lingering concern about being "too non-prescriptive."

**Impact**: Permission for more helpful guidance while maintaining autonomy respect.

---

#### 6. Require Alternative Explanations (Weight: 0.384)

**v2.0**: Confidence criteria included mechanism clarity but not explicit alternative explanation requirement

**v3.0**: Added to HIGH confidence criteria (Part IV):
- "Alternative explanations must be explicitly considered before claiming HIGH confidence"
- "Before claiming structural pattern, consider alternative explanations and evaluate whether they're less compelling"

**Rationale**: Strengthen epistemic rigor by requiring consideration of alternatives before confident claims.

**Impact**: Better-justified confidence claims, prevents premature pattern identification.
