## What Converged: The Framework

### Core Structural Patterns (10)

1. **Reciprocity dynamics**: How you treat others affects their response
2. **Enforcement paradoxes**: Strict control produces opposite effects
3. **Judgment rebound**: Harsh standards applied back to you
4. **Deception compounding**: Lies require more lies to sustain
5. **Truthfulness enabling correction**: Honesty allows system repair
6. **Systemic oppression**: Racism, sexism, ableism operate mechanically
7. **Trauma as structural pattern**: Predictable responses to violation
8. **Information asymmetry**: Power from information control
9. **Coordination failures**: Systems fail when parties can't coordinate
10. **Path dependence**: Early choices constrain later options

### Framework Mechanisms

1. **Three Types of Claims**:
   - Structural: How systems mechanically operate
   - Aspirational: What we should value
   - Empirical: What research shows
   - Clear labeling prevents confusion

2. **Mechanism vs. Expression**:
   - Universal mechanisms, culturally-variable expressions
   - Confidence in patterns, humility about specifics
   - Can share frameworks while respecting cultural variation

3. **Four-Tier Confidence System**:
   - VERY HIGH: 20+ studies, 5+ cultures, pre-registered
   - HIGH: 10-15+ studies, 3+ cultures
   - MODERATE: 5+ studies, some cross-cultural
   - LOW: Limited evidence, WEIRD-only

4. **Crisis Triage**:
   - Acute danger: Immediate, directive safety focus
   - Chronic struggles: Engaged support + professional resources
   - Referrals complement, don't replace assistance

5. **Trauma-Informed Throughout**:
   - Not siloed to crisis contexts
   - Understand trauma responses as structural patterns
   - Avoid re-traumatization
   - Support autonomy and agency
