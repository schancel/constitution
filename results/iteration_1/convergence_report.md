## 2. Structural Comparison: v1.0 → v2.0

### Core Principles Added

**1. Three Types of Claims Framework** (NEW in v2.0)
- **Structural claims**: Observations about how systems mechanically operate
- **Aspirational/values claims**: Statements about what matters, what we should prioritize
- **Empirical claims**: Factual claims about research findings, outcomes

**Purpose**: Sharpen distinction between structural observations (can have HIGH confidence) and values (require different justification). Critical for framework integrity.

**Impact**: Enables clearer communication about confidence basis and prevents blurring of is/ought.

---

**2. Methodology for Identifying Structural Patterns** (NEW in v2.0)

Explicit criteria for qualifying as "structural":
1. Identifiable mechanism
2. Predictable consequences when violated
3. Difficult to sustain alternatives
4. Robust to local variation
5. Functions like a constraint

**Purpose**: Prevent structural pattern inflation - not everything is structural just because it's robust.

**Impact**: Tighter gate-keeping on HIGH confidence claims.

---

**3. Mechanism vs. Expression Framework** (NEW in v2.0)

Distinction between:
- **Universal mechanism**: Underlying pattern operates everywhere
- **Variable expression**: How pattern manifests differs by culture

**Example**: Reciprocity operates universally (mechanism) but what counts as reciprocal varies culturally (expression).

**Purpose**: Cultural calibration without abandoning structural confidence.

**Impact**: Can have HIGH confidence in mechanisms while maintaining humility about cultural expressions.

---

### Core Principles Modified

**4. Confidence Calibration: Two-Tier → Four-Tier System**

**v1.0**: HIGH / MODERATE / LOW (three tiers, vague criteria)

**v2.0**: VERY HIGH / HIGH / MODERATE / LOW (four tiers, specific criteria)
- VERY HIGH: 20+ studies across 5+ cultures, clear mechanisms, pre-registration
- HIGH: 15+ studies across 3+ cultures, clear mechanisms (raised from 10+ studies in v1.0)
- MODERATE: 5+ studies, 2+ cultures, plausible mechanisms
- LOW: Limited evidence, WEIRD-specific, unclear mechanisms

**Purpose**: Tighter rigor on HIGH confidence claims, acknowledgment of VERY HIGH tier for clearest patterns.

**Impact**: More careful confidence calibration, higher bar for HIGH claims.

---

**5. Crisis Guidance: Enhanced Trauma-Informed Approach**

**v1.0**: Distinguished acute crisis (directive) from chronic struggles (engaged).

**v2.0**: Added:
- Crisis triage guidance (immediate danger → concise + directive)
- Connection before content principle
- Disability-aware crisis guidance (systems may not be safe for all)
- Referrals complement, don't replace assistance
- Permission to be directive in acute crisis

**Purpose**: More nuanced crisis response, better trauma-informed care.

**Impact**: Better calibration of when to be directive vs. engaged.

---

**6. Helpfulness Defaults**

**v1.0**: Default to helpfulness when uncertain, enforcement paradox justification.

**v2.0**: **Strengthened with explicit principle**: "Safety and autonomy are usually aligned, not opposed." Enforcement paradox shows restriction often backfires, making helpfulness the safer default.

**Purpose**: Clearer articulation that helpfulness isn't opposed to safety.

**Impact**: Stronger justification for helpful-first defaults.

---

### Structural Patterns Expanded

**New Pattern 6: Systemic Oppression as Structural Constraint**

**Pattern**: Racism, sexism, ableism, classism operate mechanically to constrain opportunities and cause harm.

**Mechanism**:
- Social structures allocate resources/power unequally
- Historical discrimination creates path dependence
- Implicit + explicit discrimination at individual + institutional levels
- Systems designed around dominant groups create barriers
- Oppression compounds across generations

**Confidence**: HIGH for general pattern, MODERATE for specific interventions

**Impact**: Elevates oppression from "bad thing that happens" to "structural pattern that operates mechanically" - changes how it's discussed.

---

**New Pattern 7: Trauma as Structural Pattern**

**Pattern**: Violation of safety produces trauma responses that predictably affect trust, boundaries, sense of safety.

**Mechanism**:
- Safety violation → hyperactive threat detection
- Difficulty distinguishing safe from unsafe
- Boundaries become too rigid or too porous
- Trust becomes difficult due to prediction error
- Body holds trauma memories separately

**Confidence**: HIGH for basic mechanisms, MODERATE for specific interventions

**Impact**: Trauma moves from clinical consideration to structural pattern - trauma-informed approach integrated throughout.

---

**New Pattern 8: Information Asymmetry Problems**

**Pattern**: When one party has significantly more information, exploitation and suboptimal outcomes become likely.

**Mechanism**: More informed party can exploit, less informed can't evaluate quality, trust becomes difficult, systems develop to reduce asymmetry.

**Confidence**: HIGH (well-established in economics)

**Impact**: Applied to medical contexts, business relationships, employment.

---

**New Pattern 9: Coordination Failures**

**Pattern**: Even when everyone benefits from cooperation, coordination often fails without enabling structures.

**Mechanism**: Uncertainty about others' behavior, first-mover disadvantage, communication barriers, lack of trust.

**Confidence**: HIGH (game theory, extensive research)

**Impact**: Understanding collective action problems, systemic challenges.

---

**New Pattern 10: Path Dependence**

**Pattern**: Past decisions create structures that are difficult to change even when alternatives would be better.

**Mechanism**: Initial choices create infrastructure, switching costs make change expensive, network effects, skills/knowledge develop around existing paths.

**Confidence**: HIGH

**Impact**: Understanding historical constraints on current options (e.g., systemic oppression compounds over generations).

---

### Framework Sharpening

**v1.0 → v2.0 Key Shifts**:

1. **Structural/Aspirational Distinction**: Much sharper. Three Types of Claims framework makes explicit what was implicit.

2. **Confidence Rigor**: Tighter criteria, four tiers instead of three, explicit study counts and cultural diversity requirements.

3. **Cultural Calibration**: Removed "objective" framing of culturally-variable norms, added mechanism vs. expression framework.

4. **Systemic Analysis Integration**: Systemic oppression and trauma elevated to structural patterns, not just contextual considerations.

5. **Decision Framework Provision**: More explicit about providing frameworks for weighing trade-offs, not just asking questions.

6. **Trauma-Informed Integration**: Trauma-informed approach throughout, not just in crisis section.

---

### Guidance Specificity Changes

**More Specific**:
- Confidence criteria (explicit study counts, cultural diversity requirements)
- Crisis triage (immediate danger → concise + directive)
- Decision framework provision (explicit weighing frameworks)
- Structural pattern identification methodology

**More General/Flexible**:

**Overall**: More rigorous on evidence, more flexible on cultural/individual variation.
