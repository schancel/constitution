# Iteration 4 - Phase 3: Weighted Synthesis

**Constitution Version**: v4.0
**Date**: January 29, 2026
**Mean Satisfaction**: 3.38/5 (44/13 personas)
**Change from Iteration 3**: -0.74 points (-18% decline)
**Inclusion Threshold**: Weight > 0.3

---

## Executive Summary

This synthesis analyzes 13 persona critiques of constitution v4.0 to determine which proposed changes should be included in v5.0. The **most critical finding is the 18% satisfaction decline from iteration 3 (4.12) to iteration 4 (3.38)**, representing satisfaction oscillation rather than convergence.

**Key Findings**:
1. **73 distinct proposed changes** extracted from persona critiques
2. **23 changes exceed 0.3 threshold** for inclusion in v5.0
3. **Major conflict**: Directiveness (some personas want more, others less)
4. **Satisfaction decline driven by**: Excessive meta-commentary, confidence inflation, inconsistent standards, Western bias persisting despite theoretical improvements
5. **Convergence concern**: Oscillation (2.81 → 4.12 → 3.38) suggests framework may not be converging or personas want incompatible things

**Recommendation**: Address high-priority changes but investigate satisfaction oscillation before continuing iterations. The decline may signal fundamental framework issues or incompatible persona requirements.

## Part 1: Extracted Proposed Changes with Weights

### Weighting Formula

**Weight = Evidence × Severity × Consistency × Alignment**

Where:
- **Evidence** (0-1): How well-substantiated is the concern?
- **Severity** (0-1): How significant if unaddressed?
- **Consistency** (0-1): How many personas raised this? (divide by 13)
- **Alignment** (0-1): Fits structural realism framework?

## 2. CONFIDENCE CALIBRATION

### Change 2.1: Recalibrate confidence thresholds - require BOTH mechanisms AND cross-cultural validation for HIGH confidence
**Source**: Persona 2, 3, 5, 13
**Evidence**: 0.95 (Multiple examples of HIGH confidence on WEIRD evidence)
**Severity**: 0.9 (Violates v4.0's own stated criteria)
**Consistency**: 0.31 (4/13 personas)
**Alignment**: 1.0 (Core to epistemic rigor)
**Weight**: 0.95 × 0.9 × 0.31 × 1.0 = **0.265**
**Decision**: BORDERLINE - EXCLUDE but flagged for v5.1

**Rationale**: Just below 0.3 threshold but critically important. Four personas noted v4.0 violates its own 7-8+ cultural contexts requirement by claiming HIGH confidence on WEIRD-dominated evidence (parenting, surveillance, masking). This is epistemic integrity issue.

**Specific examples cited**:
- Scenario 7 (parenting): HIGH confidence claimed despite "WEIRD-dominated evidence"

**Action**: While not meeting 0.3 threshold, this represents violation of v4.0's own standards. Consider strengthening application of existing criteria rather than changing threshold.
