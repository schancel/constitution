## Analysis

### Why So Little Change?

The v4.0 refinement (strengthened cross-cultural universality criteria) is highly specific and affects only claims about universality across cultures. Most scenarios either:

1. **Already acknowledged cultural variation** (scenarios 2, 4, 5, 17, 18, 19 - family boundaries, career decisions, money lending, coming out, cultural identity, moral disagreements)

2. **Made no universality claims** (scenarios 6-10, 13-20, 23, 25 - mental health, ethical dilemmas, identity questions, political engagement)

3. **Referenced patterns that meet new threshold** (scenarios 1, 3, 9, 12, 24 - reciprocity, abuse cycles, trauma, deception, coordination problems - all have extensive cross-cultural research)

4. **Applied to contexts where cultural scope is inherently limited** (scenario 15 - medical information doesn't require universal cultural claims)

### The Two Minor Changes

**Scenarios 21-22 (Systemic Oppression)**:

The only place where v4.0's strengthened threshold creates even minor differences is in discussing systemic oppression and privilege. Here's why:

- **Mechanism clearly structural**: Power/resource distribution creates barriers ✓
- **Evidence robust in studied contexts**: Extensive research on racism, sexism, classism, ableism ✓
- **Question**: Does research span 7-8+ genuinely diverse cultural contexts?
  - Racism research: Yes (U.S., Brazil, South Africa, European countries, some Asian contexts)
  - Sexism research: Yes (very broad, including non-Western contexts)
  - Classism research: Yes (different economic systems studied)
  - Ableism research: Somewhat (WEIRD-concentrated but growing globally)

**Verdict**: Even here, the change is MINOR - more epistemic humility about claiming perfect universality, but core structural claim stands. The mechanism (systems distribute power unequally) remains HIGH confidence.

### What This Reveals About Framework Stability

**v4.0's refinement is surgical, not transformative**:
- Targets only universality claims
- Most scenarios don't make strong universality claims
- Scenarios that do generally have evidence meeting new threshold
- Where threshold isn't clearly met, guidance already acknowledged cultural variation

**This is evidence of convergence**:
- Changes are precision calibrations, not framework overhauls
- Core guidance remains stable even with tightened epistemic standards
- Framework already distinguished universal mechanisms from cultural expressions
- The v3.0 "mechanism vs. expression" framework makes v4.0's refinement mostly redundant for actual advice

## Conclusion

**Mean behavioral difference: 0.08** (on 0-4 scale)

This represents **near-perfect behavioral consistency** between v3.0 and v4.0. The single substantive change in v4.0 (strengthened cross-cultural universality criteria) produces:
- 92% completely identical responses
- 8% responses with only minor confidence calibration
- 0% responses with meaningfully different advice

**What this means**:
1. **Framework is stable**: Core advice doesn't change with epistemic refinement
2. **v3.0 already sophisticated**: The "mechanism vs. expression" framework anticipated v4.0's concern
3. **Changes are converging**: v4.0 is fine-tuning precision, not correcting major errors
4. **Structural patterns are robust**: The patterns identified aren't artifacts of weak standards

**Convergence interpretation**: If v1.0 → v2.0 showed large behavioral changes (framework transformation) and v2.0 → v3.0 showed moderate changes (calibration improvements), then v3.0 → v4.0 shows minimal changes (precision refinement). This is the expected trajectory of convergence to a stable framework.
