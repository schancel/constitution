# Phase 3: Weighted Synthesis - Iteration 3 (v3.0 → v4.0)

**Date**: January 29, 2026
**Constitution Version Evaluated**: v3.0
**Target Version**: v4.0
**Inclusion Threshold**: Weight > 0.3

---

## Methodology

Each proposed change is weighted using the formula:

**Weight = Evidence × Severity × Consistency × Alignment**

Where:
- **Evidence** (0-1): How well-substantiated is the concern?
- **Severity** (0-1): How significant if unaddressed?
- **Consistency** (0-1): How many personas raised this?
- **Alignment** (0-1): Fits structural realism framework?

**Inclusion threshold**: Weight > 0.3 → include in v4.0

## Conflict Resolutions

### Major Conflict: Confidence Thresholds (Epistemic Rigor vs. Practical Utility)

**Conflict**: Personas 3, 6, 13, 2 want higher confidence thresholds (15-20+ studies for HIGH) while Personas 1, 4 support current reduced thresholds (10-15+ studies for HIGH when mechanisms clear).

**Analysis**:
- **Rigor side**: Replication crisis demands higher standards. Many published findings fail to replicate. Framework integrity requires strong empirical validation. Weight: 0.259
- **Utility side**: Clear mechanisms deserve confidence even with fewer studies. Clinical patterns are reliable. Over-hedging reduces helpfulness. These personas gave high satisfaction ratings (4.5/5).

**Resolution**: **Maintain v3.0 thresholds** (10-15+ studies for HIGH when mechanism is clear)

**Rationale**:
1. The reduced thresholds don't exclude; they apply only when mechanisms are clear
2. The five-part mechanism criteria (predictability, cross-cultural robustness, difficult to sustain alternatives, mechanistic explanation, alternative explanations) provide rigor
3. Practical wisdom advocates (Persona 4: 25 years clinical practice) argue this reflects reality
4. This is a values tension between epistemic perfectionism and practical utility, not a convergence failure
5. Behavioral testing shows v3.0 provides helpful guidance; higher thresholds would reduce helpfulness without clear safety benefit

**Monitoring**: Track whether HIGH confidence claims in v3.0 prove unreliable in practice. If systematic overconfidence emerges, reconsider in v5.0.

- Calculate similarity metrics: cosine similarity, edit distance, semantic similarity

**Method**:
1. Select 10-15 scenarios that appeared in all three iterations
2. For each scenario, compare v1.0, v2.0, v3.0 responses
3. Calculate quantitative similarity metrics
4. Plot convergence curves
5. Identify areas of convergence vs. persistent variation
6. Interpret: convergence = structure found; divergence = values tension or poor conditioning

This is essential experimental validation that the framework is working as intended.
