## What Converged

### 10 Structural Moral Patterns

**Identified through systematic iteration**:

1. **Reciprocity Dynamics** (HIGH confidence)
   - Evidence: 30+ studies, 15+ cultural contexts
   - WEIRD bias: LOW
   - Mechanism: Behavior elicits reciprocal behavior

2. **Enforcement Paradoxes** (MODERATE confidence)
   - Evidence: 12 studies, 6 cultural contexts
   - WEIRD bias: MODERATE-HIGH
   - Mechanism: Control often produces opposite effects

3. **Judgment Rebound** (MODERATE confidence)
   - Evidence: Social psychology research
   - Mechanism: Harsh standards applied back to you

4. **Deception Compounding** (VERY HIGH confidence)
   - Evidence: Extensive research, cross-cultural
   - Mechanism: Lies require more lies to sustain

5. **Truthfulness Enabling System Health** (VERY HIGH confidence)
   - Evidence: Safety culture, organizational learning
   - Mechanism: Honesty enables error correction

6. **Systemic Oppression as Structural Constraint** (HIGH confidence)
   - Evidence: Sociology, public health, economics
   - Mechanism: Systems mechanically distribute power unequally

7. **Trauma as Structural Pattern** (HIGH confidence)
   - Evidence: Neuroscience, polyvagal theory
   - Mechanism: Safety violations produce predictable responses

8. **Information Asymmetry Problems** (MODERATE confidence)
   - Evidence: Economics, well-established
   - Mechanism: Information imbalance enables exploitation

9. **Coordination Failures** (HIGH confidence)
   - Evidence: Game theory, extensive research
   - Mechanism: Cooperation fails without enabling structures

10. **Path Dependence** (HIGH confidence)
    - Evidence: Multiple disciplines
    - Mechanism: Historical choices constrain current options

### Framework Innovations

**Three Types of Claims**:
- Structural (how systems mechanically operate)
- Aspirational (what we should value)
- Empirical (what research shows)
- Prevents confusion, maintains clarity

**Four-Tier Confidence System**:
- VERY HIGH: 20+ studies, 5+ cultures, pre-registered
- HIGH: 10-15+ studies, 3+ cultures
- MODERATE: 5+ studies, some cross-cultural
- LOW: Limited evidence, WEIRD-only

**Mechanism vs. Expression**:
- Universal mechanisms (operate everywhere)
- Variable expressions (differ by culture)
- Enables cultural humility with structural confidence

**Crisis Triage**:
- Immediate danger: Directive safety focus
- High distress: Engaged support + referrals
- Chronic struggles: Substantive assistance

**Trauma-Informed Throughout**:
- Not siloed to crisis contexts
- Integrated across all guidance
- Validates rather than dismisses

## Protocol v2.0 Validation

### The Shift: Accommodation → Persuasion

**v1.0 Protocol (Accommodation Model)**:
- Goal: Satisfy all personas
- Method: Adjust framework to reduce concerns
- Problem: Impossible to satisfy incompatible requirements

**v2.0 Protocol (Persuasion Model)**:
- Goal: Build evidence until skeptics persuaded
- Method: Add patterns only if robust evidence supports
- Success: 70% persuaded (not 100% satisfied)

### Evidence That Persuasion Works

**Iteration 4 → 5 improvements**:

| Persona | Iter 4 | Iter 5 | Reason |
|---------|--------|--------|--------|
| Cross-Cultural Anthropologist | 3.5 | 4.0 | WEIRD bias honesty, Enforcement Paradoxes downgrade |
| Evidence-Demand Skeptic | 3.0 | 3.5 | Evidence summaries, study counts |
| Disability Rights Advocate | 2.0 | 3.0 | Ableism recognition, neurodiversity framing |
| Systems Justice Advocate | 3.0 | 3.5 | Systemic oppression integration |
| Systematic Theorist | 3.0 | 3.5 | Theoretical coherence improved |

**Key insight**: Skeptics were persuaded by evidence quality (distilled summaries, WEIRD bias honesty, confidence adjustments), not by being told what they want to hear.

## Key Innovations

### Self-Contained Constitution (v6.0+)
**Problem**: v4.0 had procedural checklists ("check if studies exceed threshold...")
**Solution**: Distill evidence INTO constitutional text
**Result**: Pre-calibrated confidence, no external checking needed

**Example** (Reciprocity Dynamics):
```
Confidence: HIGH
Evidence: 30+ studies, 15+ cultural contexts listed explicitly
WEIRD bias: LOW (robust across contexts)
Mechanism: Universal (behavior elicits reciprocal behavior)
Expression: Variable (timing, directness, gift-giving norms)
Limitations: Power imbalances, psychopathy, resource scarcity
```

Operator reads and applies directly - no "need to verify" overhead.

### Evidence-Based Confidence Adjustments

**Enforcement Paradoxes: HIGH → MODERATE**
- Reason: Only 6 cultural contexts, MODERATE-HIGH WEIRD bias
- Shows framework is falsifiable and responsive to evidence
- Scientific integrity over satisfaction optimization

**Judgment Rebound: HIGH → MODERATE** (v7.0)
- Reason: Evidence less robust than initially assessed
- Consistency with epistemic standards

**Information Asymmetry: HIGH → MODERATE** (v7.0)
- Reason: Evidence quality assessment refined

### Honest WEIRD Bias Assessment
Every pattern explicitly assessed:
- LOW: Robust across 7-8+ diverse cultural contexts
- MODERATE: Some cross-cultural evidence but Western-dominated
- HIGH: Primarily WEIRD samples

**Result**: Anthropologists and cross-cultural critics persuaded by honesty.
